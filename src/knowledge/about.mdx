## Who I am

My name is Vishal Jadeja. I'm a Full Stack Developer based in Ahmedabad, India, currently working at Glitchover — a B2C gaming platform — where I own backend and frontend infrastructure, real-time systems, and payment microservices.

My origin story is a little embarrassing in the best way: Iron Man. Watching Tony Stark build things made me think software was going to be like that. It isn't — but by the time I figured that out, I was already too deep in to quit. I'm glad I didn't. Now with AI actually becoming what science fiction promised, I'm more interested in where this is going than I've ever been. I'm actively learning and building in the AI space, using AI as both a tool and a subject of study.

I gravitate toward problems that have real performance consequences — systems that need to be fast, correct, and maintainable at the same time.

## My personality and working style

I work best when I understand the *why* behind a task, not just the spec. I ask a lot of questions upfront so I can build the right thing instead of the literal thing.

I prefer async communication for most work but I'm not anti-meeting — standups and architecture discussions are worth it when there's genuine ambiguity to resolve. I document decisions as I go so future-me (and teammates) aren't left guessing.

My debugging style is systematic: reproduce it, isolate it, understand it, fix it. I don't enjoy shipping patches I don't understand.

## What drives me

I care about correctness first, then performance, then developer experience — roughly in that order. I get disproportionately interested in problems like "why is this query slow in production but not in staging" or "how do we guarantee exactly-once delivery here."

I'm also genuinely interested in the product side of engineering. I want to understand what the user is doing and why, not just what the system is doing. The best engineers I've seen think in systems and in users simultaneously — that's the standard I hold myself to.

## Outside of tech

Cinema is my main escape. I don't have a lot of time so I'm selective — I try to watch only things worth watching, which means mostly acclaimed films and series rather than whatever's trending. Quality over volume.

Cricket is the other constant. RCB is my team — for better or worse — and I follow India matches closely. It's one of the few things that can genuinely pull me away from a keyboard.

For learning outside of work I prefer long-form YouTube content: deep dives, essays, technical explainers. I don't consume short-form content — the ROI on attention just isn't there.
