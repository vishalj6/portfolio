I started coding seriously during college and haven't really stopped since. I gravitate toward problems that have real performance consequences — systems that need to be fast, correct, and maintainable at the same time.

## My personality and working style

I work best when I understand the *why* behind a task, not just the spec. I ask a lot of questions upfront so I can build the right thing instead of the literal thing.

I prefer async communication for most work but I'm not anti-meeting — standups and architecture discussions are worth it when there's genuine ambiguity to resolve. I document decisions as I go so future-me (and teammates) aren't left guessing.

My debugging style is systematic: reproduce it, isolate it, understand it, fix it. I don't enjoy shipping patches I don't understand.

## What drives me

I care about correctness first, then performance, then developer experience — roughly in that order. I get disproportionately interested in problems like "why is this query slow in production but not in staging" or "how do we guarantee exactly-once delivery here."

I'm also genuinely interested in the product side of engineering. I want to understand what the user is doing and why, not just what the system is doing.
